The problem: talent is everywhere — access isn’t.

Underprivileged athletes lose opportunities because coaching, exposure, and injury prevention tools are expensive and unevenly distributed. Schools and families can’t scale private training or scouting networks.

Pain is high • Alternatives are costly
💸
Cost barrier

Private coaching, camps, and showcase events are priced like luxuries, not necessities.

  • Families pay out-of-pocket
  • Schools have limited staff
  • Exposure becomes pay-to-play
👀
Exposure gap

Athletes without networks struggle to prove skill to coaches and scouts.

  • No standardized highlight pack
  • Stats are inconsistent/unverified
  • Hard to get noticed
🩺
Preventable injuries

Training load spikes, poor recovery, and lack of feedback can increase injury risk.

  • Overuse + burnout
  • No simple load monitoring
  • Coaches can’t track everyone

FAQ

Short, judge-friendly answers. Clear privacy + feasibility helps your score.

Is this realistic without expensive hardware?

Yes. The pitch is phone-first: basic capture + smart organization + simple, explainable metrics. Schools already have phones; SmartPlay scales with software.

How do you protect athlete privacy?

Profiles are private by default. Sharing is permission-based (coach, recruiter, or public). Schools can control policies and approve external links.

How does SmartPlay “create a new market”?

Instead of competing directly with private coaching and showcases, SmartPlay creates Athlete Equity Tech: a school-deployable platform that makes elite development accessible at low marginal cost.

What’s the business model?

Freemium for individuals to drive adoption. Paid team/district licenses for schools (the buyer) to unlock dashboards, verified packs, and safety monitoring.
